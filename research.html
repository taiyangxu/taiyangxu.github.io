Research Interests

I'm an analyst who place the particular interests in the area of mathematical physics and applied mathematics. More specifically, I am interested in the integrable systems (IS) as well as related topics, which include

  • Exactly solvable solutions of integrable PDEs.

  • The scattering theory and spectral analysis for integrable PDEs.

  • The long-time and asymptotics for the Cauchy problem of the integrable PDEs, including both zero and nonzero background.

Recently, I am also trying to enter a new field which is closely related to IS, namely, random matrices theory (RMT). I'm particularly attracted by

  • Determinantal point process (DPP).

  • Integrable kernels and related Fredholm determinant theory.

  • Analysis of gap, distribution and correlations functions.

The application of asymptotic methods, special functions, PDE analysis as well as complex analytical technique is the central to the work. Riemann-Hilbert (RH) problem is the main tool used by me to study physically meaningful quantities arising in the fields of integrable differential equations and random matrices theory, in particular their asymptotics associated to special functions and Painlev\'e family equations.
